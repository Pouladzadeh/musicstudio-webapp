    • About me

    Nastaran Pouladzadeh was born in 1977 in Iran where she studied composition, piano, Iranian music. In 2009 she took her master of music(composition) in the department of music art university of Tehran with a thesis (composition using Iranian ancient music). Her teachers included Kiavash Saheb Nassagh, Nader Mashayekhi, Fouziye Majd, Amir Eslami. She has a particular interest in contemporary music and Iranian ancient music and has composed many instrumental pieces for different ensembles and orchestras such as Porkhan: a piece for orchestra and Iranian musical instruments and tape, Zanan Ghalibaf (carpet or rug knitting women): a piece for orchestra and electronic sounds and vocal, Afarinesh(Creation) : a piece for orchestra and vocal and ... . After moving to Toronto Canada she is working on electroacoustic music.

    Teaching Philosophy

    The primary goal of my teaching is to make the study of piano and playing music engaging, fulfilling and fun; to give my students a lifelong appreciation of music in the process.
    My philosophy of education is that all students are unique and have something special that they can bring to their own ​learning journey​.
    I will assist my students to express and accept themselves for who they are, as well to accept their differences with others.
    As students progress, I encourage them to develop their own sense of musicianship through thinking about structure, musical style, and emotional content of the music they are playing.
